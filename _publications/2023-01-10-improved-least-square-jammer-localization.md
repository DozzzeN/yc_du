### Contributions & Highlights
* **Stochastic Geometry Integration:** Developed a novel localization framework that overcomes the limitations of direct interference measurement by exploiting **Stochastic Geometry Theory**. This approach analyzes coverage variations of wireless devices surrounding a jammer to refine ranging estimations.
* **Enhanced Least-Square Framework:** Engineered an improved **Least-Square (LS)** localization algorithm that utilizes network distribution properties. By incorporating the expected value of listening distances, the method effectively mitigates the inaccuracies inherent in traditional distance-based estimation.
* **Significant Precision Gains:** Demonstrated through extensive numerical evaluations, the proposed method achieves a **35% reduction in mean localization error** compared to traditional LS methods, ensuring robust performance across diverse interference scenarios.
